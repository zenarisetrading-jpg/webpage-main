Where Intentwise Falls Short

Intentwise is a capable analytics platform. The limitation is a fundamental one: analytics describes history, it doesn't prove causation.

Gap #1

Analytics ≠ Causal Proof

Intentwise shows you what happened after your changes. But "sales improved after we made this change" is correlation, not causation. Without a counterfactual baseline, you don't know if your decision caused the result or just preceded it.

Gap #2

Market Noise Pollutes Results

Amazon PPC results are noisy — seasonality, competitor stock-outs, ranking fluctuations all move your numbers. Analytics tools report the numbers. Only counterfactual measurement separates your signal from the market's noise.

Gap #3

No Decision-Level Tracking

Intentwise aggregates performance at the campaign and account level. It doesn't link individual optimization decisions — a specific bid change, a negative keyword addition — to the revenue outcomes that followed them.

and Actionability

Intentwise provides beautiful, detailed dashboards and deep analytics for Amazon Advertising. For data-hungry teams, it's a powerful tool for visualizing high-level performance trends. However, there is often a significant gap between **seeing the data** and **verifying the specific impact** of your subsequent manual or automated actions.

AdPulse is built to close that gap. While Intentwise shows you what happened across your account, AdPulse shows you what happened **because** of your specific optimizations. We move beyond general trend analysis to granular decision verification, using counterfactual models to prove the ROI of every bid adjustment. Knowing your ACOS is down is nice; knowing which exact negative keyword harvest caused it is a competitive advantage.
