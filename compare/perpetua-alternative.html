Why Yield-First Beats
Bid-First Automation

Automation tools like Perpetua are designed to optimize for a target ROAS or ACoS. They do this by moving bids up and down until the math matches your goal—a process often called 'bid-first' automation. But this approach has a structural flaw: it assumes every sale within that attribution window was caused solely by the bid change. **It ignores the counterfactual.**

AdPulse is yield-first. We don't just chase a ROAS target; we verify the marginal yield of every dollar spent. If a keyword is hitting its ROAS goal but would have converted anyway at a lower bid due to high brand resonance, AdPulse surfaces that waste. Perpetua's automation keeps spending because the ACOS "looks good."

Furthermore, Perpetua's "Optimizers" are often black boxes. You see the bid change, but never the rationale. AdPulse provides a **Confidence Score** and a **Forecast Delta** for every recommendation. We believe that for high-growth brands, understanding the *why* is just as important as the *what*.
