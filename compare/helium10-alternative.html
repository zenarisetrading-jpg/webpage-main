Gap #1

A Feature, Not a Focus

Adtomic is one component of a 30+ feature platform built around keyword research and listing tools. PPC attribution requires deep focus. When it's a feature, it gets feature-level depth — not product-level depth.

Gap #2

Standard Metrics, No Incrementality

Adtomic shows ACoS, ROAS, clicks, and conversions — the same metrics you get from Amazon itself. What it doesn't show is whether your specific bid or keyword changes actually caused the results, or if the market moved anyway.

The Accountability Ceiling of All-in-One Tools

Helium 10 is the gold standard for finding products and optimizing listings. But when you use an all-in-one tool for PPC, you eventually hit an accountability ceiling. Because the tool is broad, its PPC features (Adtomic) focus on execution — pushing bids and harvesting keywords — rather than verification.

AdPulse was built to solve the verification problem. We don't just help you manage ads; we help you prove that your management actually worked. By isolating the impact of every decision, we turn "I think this keyword helped" into "I know this keyword added $412 in revenue."

In Adtomic, you're looking at a standard ACOS dashboard. In AdPulse, you're looking at a **Decision Log**. Every bid change, every search term blocked, and every keyword harvested is recorded and followed for 60 days. We then apply counterfactual modelling (comparing your results to a synthetic market baseline) to show you the true incremental growth your PPC optimizations produced.

Why specialized attribution wins:

  • ✓
    Multi-Horizon Attribution: Standard tools look at 7 or 14-day attribution. AdPulse tracks every decision through 14, 30, and 60-day windows to capture longer conversion cycles.
  • ✓
    Synthentic Market Baselines: We subtract "market noise" from your results. If a category-wide wave lifted your sales, we won't let you mistakenly take credit for it.
  • ✓
    Decision rationale logging: Every AdPulse optimization comes with a 'Reason Code,' making client reporting or internal reviews a 2-minute task.
