Getting Started

Do I need API access to use AdPulse?

No. AdPulse works by analyzing your Amazon Ads reports directly. Just download your Search Term Report from Amazon Ads and upload it to AdPulse. No API connection required.

Is my data secure?

Yes. Your data is processed locally in your browser. We never store your raw data on our servers. The only data we retain (with your permission) is anonymized performance metrics to improve our algorithms.

How long does setup take?

About 10 minutes. Download your report from Amazon Ads, upload it to AdPulse, and you'll see recommendations immediately. No training required.

How It Works

What is "counterfactual analysis"?

Counterfactual analysis calculates what would have happened if you hadn't made a change. By comparing actual results to this baseline, we can separate the impact of your decisions from market trends and noise.

Why do you measure at 14/30/60 days instead of 7?

Amazon's attribution window is slow—especially for higher-priced products. Measuring at 7 days captures only about 75% of conversions. Our multi-horizon measurement ensures you see the full impact of your decisions.

How is this different from other PPC tools?

Most tools help you make changes faster. AdPulse is the only tool that proves which changes actually worked. We track every decision and measure its real-world impact, separating your skill from market luck. See the full comparison →

Impact Measurement

How do you calculate "Decision Impact"?

We calculate what your target would have earned based on account-level trends (the baseline). Then we compare actual performance to this baseline. The difference is your Decision Impact—the incremental revenue attributable to your specific change.

Can I trust the numbers?

Every impact claim passes normalized validation against market baseline. We show confidence scores and flag uncertain results. No guessing, no false positives. You see exactly how we calculated each number.

What if a decision hurt performance?

We show you that too. AdPulse tracks both wins and losses, so you learn what doesn't work for your account. This feedback loop is what helps you improve over time.
